1. Executive Summary

Allting is being formed as a standalone entity in Meydan Free Zone, Dubai, to operate a shared software platform that enables AI operating companies (OpCos) to launch and scale in emerging markets. The platform is already running in production under the founding team's existing ExCom AI L.L.C-FZ entity and will be migrated/contributed into Allting upon incorporation.

The platform earns a recurring revenue share from every OpCo on it. The thesis is that AI infrastructure, like telecom infrastructure before it, is in the early stage of de-layering — and that the operator/platform split that produced companies like Stripe, Adyen, and American Tower in adjacent industries is the most defensible position in the AI value chain for emerging markets.

3. Business Model & Unit Economics

Software platform with infrastructure-grade economics.

Revenue model

Allting earns a recurring share of every OpCo's revenue (guidance range ~12–15%, expected to compress to ~10–12% in negotiated enterprise deals). The platform absorbs token costs (the variable COGS) and passes finished services to OpCos through a single API gateway, a managed billing/security/compliance layer, and a tenant-isolated runtime.

4. Market Context

Three structural shifts that we believe make this thesis credible over a 5–10 year horizon, not a cyclical bet:

  • AI inference cost has fallen ~200–300x from GPT-4 launch (March 2023) to mid-2026 (TokenCost, DeepLearning.ai). This crosses the viability threshold for emerging-market price points.
  • Open-source has reached production parity. Meta's Llama ecosystem alone exceeds 1.2B cumulative downloads (Meta, 2026). On-premises and self-hosted now controls a majority share of the deployed LLM market in enterprise.
  • Adoption growth is highest where AI is scarcest. Microsoft's AI Diffusion Report 2025 finds ChatGPT adoption growth in low-income countries is ~4x higher than in high-income countries. The 10.6pp North–South adoption gap is widening, not narrowing.

Demand is proven and growing. The bottleneck is operator-grade infrastructure to serve it at viable price points — which is the gap Allting fills.

7. Self-Assessment of Risks

Honest reading of what could go wrong, and how we think about it.

Corporate status — entity not yet formed

Allting itself is not yet incorporated. The platform currently operates under ExCom AI L.L.C-FZ. Series A close requires incorporation in Meydan Free Zone, definition of the cap table among the founding team, and migration/contribution of platform IP. This is a known and time-boxed workstream — not a structural risk — but it must be completed before any close.

Take rate compression

Guidance is 12–15% revenue share. Enterprise OpCos will negotiate. Realistic realised rate is likely 10–12% at scale, modelled accordingly.

Onboarding cost is not zero

"Near-zero marginal cost per new OpCo" is true for infrastructure (multi-tenant compute, shared services). Onboarding, integration support, and account management scale linearly — not exponentially. This is reflected in the cost model.

Hyperscaler / gateway competition

AWS Bedrock, Azure AI Foundry, Google Vertex AI compete on raw model access. OpenRouter ($500M valuation, $100M+ ARR) competes on developer-facing routing. None operate the operator/revenue-share model in emerging markets. Allting's moat is ecosystem, distribution and regional operating expertise — not the gateway itself.

OpCo concentration today

A meaningful share of current user volume comes from one consumer OpCo (Sport vertical). Phase 1 mitigates this through diversified pipeline: five external operators (BioTech, E-commerce, Finance, Compliance, Marketplace) in onboarding.

Regulatory exposure across emerging markets

Operating across LatAm and MENA exposes the platform to evolving AI regulation. The platform is GDPR-compliant today. SOC 2 and regional certifications are on the Phase 1 roadmap.

No debt, no SAFEs, no convertibles

Clean cap table. Once formed, the entity starts free of any prior instruments. New investors are pricing entry, not legacy structure.
